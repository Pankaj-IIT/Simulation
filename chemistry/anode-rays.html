Anode Rays & Properties

Discharge Tube with Perforated Cathode (Canal Rays)

Properties of Anode Rays (Canal Rays)

Positive charge: Deflected toward negative plate in electric field
Massive particles: Mass depends on gas in tube (same as positive ion)
Variable e/m ratio: Depends on the gas used in the tube
Slow speed: Much slower than cathode rays (1/100th to 1/1000th)
Pass through holes: Pass through perforations (canals) in cathode
Ionize gas: Can ionize gas molecules
Proton discovery: For hydrogen gas, rays are protons (H⁺ ions)

Cathode Rays (e⁻)

• Negative charge

• Very light (electron mass)

• Constant e/m ratio

• Very high speed

• Produced from gas ionization

Anode Rays (Positive Ions)

• Positive charge

• Heavy (ion mass)

• Variable e/m ratio

• Low speed

• Pass through cathode holes

Tube Controls

Anode Rays (Canal Rays) discovered by Goldstein (1886). They are positive ions produced when gas molecules lose electrons. The rays pass through holes (canals) in the perforated cathode.
