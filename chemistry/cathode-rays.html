Cathode Rays & Properties

Discharge Tube (Low Pressure Gas ~0.01 mmHg)

Properties of Cathode Rays

Travel in straight lines: Cast in shadows of objects placed in their path
Negative charge: Deflected by electric & magnetic fields toward positive plate
Mass: Each particle has mass equal to 1/1836th of hydrogen atom (electron)
Kinetic energy: Possess kinetic energy, can rotate a paddle wheel
Fluorescence: Produce fluorescence on hitting glass or certain materials
Penetration: Can penetrate through thin metal foils
Ionization: Can ionize gas molecules through which they pass

Tube Controls

Cathode Rays are streams of electrons observed in vacuum tubes. Discovered by J.J. Thomson (1897). They travel from cathode (-) to anode (+).
